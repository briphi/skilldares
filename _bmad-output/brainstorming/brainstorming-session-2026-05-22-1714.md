**Facilitator:** Briphi

## Session Overview

**Topic:** Skilldares — a static web quiz app for Kildares staff to learn/test on the Kildares menu.

**Goals:** Generate a high volume of raw product/feature ideas. Quantity-first divergent ideation, target 100+ ideas before any organization or filtering.

### Session Setup

- **Audience for the product:** **New Kildares pub staff** (first-week / pre-shift-ready hires). Narrowed mid-session from a multi-persona app — see "Scope Decision" below.

### Scope Decision (mid-session, 2026-05-22)

User narrowed audience from "all staff" to **new staff only**. Ideas #11–#30 (Veteran Bartender "Liam" + Manager/Owner "Siobhan") were generated before this decision and are **out of scope** — preserved below for record only, not for prioritization. Phase 2 (SCAMPER) and Phase 3 (Cross-Pollination) will focus exclusively on new-staff onboarding pain.
- **Form factor:** Static web application (no backend server required).
- **Core function:** Quiz staff on the Kildares menu — food, drinks, prices, ingredients, allergens, descriptions, specials.
- **Facilitator mode:** Push for divergent volume; pivot creative domain every ~10 ideas to fight semantic clustering; chase the ideas in the 50–100 range, not just the obvious first 20.

## Technique Selection

**Approach:** AI-Recommended Techniques

**Sequence:**
1. **Role Playing** *(collaborative)* — Generate from multiple staff personas (first-week server, veteran bartender, kitchen prep, manager) to ground divergence in real user contexts.
2. **SCAMPER Method** *(structured)* — Apply seven orthogonal lenses (Substitute, Combine, Adapt, Modify, Put to other uses, Eliminate, Reverse) to force systematic variation of the quiz concept.
3. **Cross-Pollination** *(creative)* — Import mechanics from outside the "training app" box: Duolingo, Anki, fitness apps, games, social apps, escape rooms.

**Rationale:** Sequence moves from grounded user needs → systematic structural variation → novel mechanic imports. Combats semantic clustering at every phase shift.

### Phase 1: Role Playing — Persona 1: First-Week Server "Erin" (IN SCOPE)

Context: Just hired. Knows nothing. Manager wants her shadow-shift-ready in 7 days. Terrified of getting orders wrong, mispronouncing things, or freezing at the table.

- **#1. Menu-Section Drill** — Quiz "which section is the Brisket Fries in?" Forces section-mapping recall, how veterans navigate menus.
- **#2. Price-Ladder Quiz** — Rank 4 items cheapest → most expensive. Relative pricing knowledge for upsell judgment.
- **#3. Pronunciation Trainer** — Tap item, hear it pronounced. Audio-only items where Erin must pick the written match. Reuben, Bangers, Halloumi, Casamigos, Bitburger.
- **#4. "What's In It?" Speed Round** — 10 seconds to name 3 ingredients in a dish. Real-shift response time.
- **#5. Allergen Lifeguard Mode** — "Guest is GF. Of these 6 items, which can they eat?" Stakes-weighted scoring; allergen errors aren't equal.
- **#6. Suggest a Pairing** — Show dish, pick best beer/cocktail pairing. Cross-menu upsell instinct.
- **#7. Confidence Phrases Generator** — "How would you describe the Dubliner Burger?" Picks among 3 phrasings; trains delivery, not just facts.
- **#8. Photo → Name Flashcards** — See plated dish, name it. Visual recognition for runners.
- **#9. Modifier Memory** — "All burgers can be made with…?" Trains hidden footnote modifiers.
- **#10. Section Speedrun Streak** — Time-attack: name every item in "Shareables" before timer ends. Streak counter.

### Phase 2: SCAMPER on "menu quiz for new server" (IN SCOPE)

## Idea Organization — Thematic Clusters (In-Scope Ideas Only)

### Theme 1: Question Mechanics & Content Types
*The actual quiz formats. The "what does a question look like."*
**Ideas:** #1, #2, #4, #6, #8, #9, #33, #35, #40, #58, #61, #62, #64, #65, #84, #85
**Insight:** A wide range of question shapes were generated — from straight recall (#1, #4) to inversions (#61, #62, #64) to assembly tasks (#85). The standouts deviate from multiple-choice: free-text/voice (#58, #32), reverse-direction (#61, #64), and guest-context framing (#64) all train closer to real-shift cognition than standard quizzes.

### Theme 2: Verbal Delivery & Pronunciation
*Speaking and hearing, not just tapping.*
**Ideas:** #3, #7, #32, #34, #93
**Insight:** Servers don't *know* the menu — they *speak* it. This cluster trains the modality where mistakes actually happen. #34 (vet voice memos) and #93 (record-your-own + compare) are uniquely tied to the Kildares team identity.

### Theme 3: Allergen Safety (Stakes-Weighted Mode)
*The category where mistakes are categorically different.*
**Ideas:** #5, #46, #49, #65
**Insight:** Most quiz apps treat all questions equally. The allergen category is asymmetric — one mistake = guest hospitalized. This theme uniformly treats it as a separate mode, not just a question type.

### Theme 4: Pedagogy Engine — SRS, Mastery, Curriculum
*The learning algorithm under the hood.*
**Ideas:** #41, #44, #66, #67, #69, #70, #75, #94
**Insight:** This is where the app earns its keep. SRS (#41) is the highest-leverage idea in the whole session — it makes everything else more effective. Mastery progression (#44, #66) gives Erin a visible map of where she stands.

### Theme 5: Session Shapes & Time Contexts
*When and how long a session is. Where it fits in Erin's day.*
**Ideas:** #31, #45, #47, #48, #50, #57, #73, #78, #88, #95
**Insight:** New hires don't have 30-minute study blocks. They have 60-second pre-shift windows (#50), 15-minute commutes (#31), Wordle-moment morning rituals (#45). The session shape is part of the product.

### Theme 6: Real-Shift Simulation
*Pressure-tested integration of all knowledge.*
**Ideas:** #40, #81, #87, #89
**Insight:** Item-level quizzes test recall; shift sims test orchestration. #87 is the integration test the rest of the app feeds into. #89 (replay mistakes) is the reflection layer that's missing from most quiz apps.

### Theme 7: Gamification & Motivation Loops
*Why Erin comes back tomorrow.*
**Ideas:** #43, #71, #72, #76, #77, #82, #83, #86, #79
**Insight:** Onboarding has a 7-day window. The product needs to be opened daily without manager nagging. Personal-best (#77) and rings (#76) work solo; #71 (Pokédex) makes the menu feel like territory to conquer.

### Theme 8: Coworker / Light Social Layer
*Even with solo focus, social cues drive engagement.*
**Ideas:** #34, #45, #68, #74, #92
**Insight:** Kept small intentionally — scope is new-staff-only. But mnemonic trading (#74) and vet voice memos (#34) import institutional knowledge without requiring full social features.

### Theme 9: Mnemonics & Memory Techniques
*Helping the content stick.*
**Ideas:** #90, #91
**Insight:** #90 (pub-map memory palace) is the most Kildares-specific idea in the whole session — only works *because* the staff knows the physical pub. Unique competitive moat.

### Theme 10: Multi-Use Repurposing
*Same data, more places.*
**Ideas:** #39, #51, #52, #53, #54, #55
**Insight:** The menu content is reusable. #39 (training + reference) and #53 (on-shift cheat glance) are the highest-value repurposes — they keep the app open all day, not just during study. #55 (forkable template) is the long-game play.

### Theme 11: Real-World Integration
*Physical pub, printed menu, paper, no screens.*
**Ideas:** #36, #37, #38, #59
**Insight:** #36 (QR on the printed menu) is the killer entry-point — it bridges the analog menu Erin already holds with the digital trainer. Lowest friction onboarding step.

### Theme 12: Reflection & Self-Assessment Layers
*Looking back, not just forward.*
**Ideas:** #62, #79, #86, #89
**Insight:** Distinct from grading. These let Erin *judge her own* understanding (#79), *teach back* what she knows (#62), and *re-examine mistakes* (#89). High pedagogical value, often missing from quiz apps.

## Breakthrough Concepts (Highest-Leverage Ideas)

These ideas combine novelty, feasibility for a static web app, and direct hit on first-week-server pain:

1. **#5 Allergen Lifeguard Mode** — Stakes-weighted scoring on the one category that triggers real-world consequences. Single biggest ops value per pixel of app.
2. **#41 Spaced Repetition Engine** — Multiplier on everything else. Without SRS, the app is a flashcard set. With it, it's a learning system.
3. **#36 QR-On-The-Menu Bridge** — Physical → digital onramp with zero install friction. The printed menu becomes the index into the app.
4. **#50 Pre-Shift 5-Question Warm-Up** — Embeds training in the existing pre-shift moment. No new habit needed.
5. **#90 Pub-Map Memory Palace** — Uniquely Kildares-specific; can't be copied by a generic training app.
6. **#39 Allergen-Card Combo (Training + Reference)** — One app for both study and on-shift lookup. Multiplies daily usage.
7. **#61 Reverse Pictionary** — Erin describes; app guesses. Trains the actual server output, not just inputs.
8. **#56 No-Account Mode** — Aligns perfectly with the static-site constraint. localStorage-only, zero backend.
